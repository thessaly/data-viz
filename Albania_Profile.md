## Section B1 – Promotion of the UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science

### B1.1 — Institutions involved in promoting the Recommendation

The 2021 UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science has been promoted and shared by key institutions: the Ministry of Education and Sport (MoES), the National Agency for Scientific Research and Innovation (NASRI), the Academy of Sciences (ASA), the Agency for Innovation and Excellence (AIE), and Higher Education Institutions (HEIs). These institutions are working to align Albania's science and innovation policies with UNESCO's open science principles.

MoES has integrated open science into the National Strategy for Science, Technology, and Innovation (2023–2030) and into Law No. 109/2024 "On science and scientific research in Republic of Albania". MoES also encourages participation in Horizon Europe. NASRI manages national research funds and has supported projects applying open access publishing and data sharing. The Academy of Sciences has hosted workshops and discussions on open science best practices. AIE supports open-access platforms, research centres, and innovation hubs. Higher Education Institutions have engaged through dedicated meetings and round tables.

### B1.2 — Language(s) of promotion activities

Albanian Language.

### B1.3 — Awareness-raising activities organized or foreseen

Several institutions have conducted awareness-raising activities aligned with the UNESCO 2021 Recommendation. MoES has supported round tables focusing on open access publications and open science infrastructures. NASRI, in collaboration with MoES, has organized Open Science Seminars and Training Sessions with academic staff and researchers. The Academy of Sciences has published reports on open scientific knowledge and open access publications. AIE hosts innovation hubs and workshops integrating open-source software and open hardware practices. HEIs have organized seminars and round tables for students and faculty on open access publishing, open educational resources (OER), and societal engagement in research.

### B1.4 — Actions taken to incorporate open science values into publicly funded research

MoES has aligned its National Strategy for Science, Technology, and Innovation (2023–2030) with open science principles, emphasizing accessibility of publicly funded research outputs. NASRI integrates open science principles into its research funding criteria — grant applicants are encouraged to follow open access and data sharing principles. The Academy of Sciences facilitates public dialogue between researchers and society, encouraging the use of open-access platforms. AIE supports the development of open-source software and open hardware by publicly funded researchers. The Technology Park is developing platforms that allow publicly funded data to be freely shared and accessed.

## Section B2 – Open Science Policies and Governance

### B2.1–2.2 — National open science policy / legal instrument

Albania's primary legal instrument is **Law No. 109/2024 "On science and scientific research in Republic of Albania"**, approved in 2024. It aims to modernize the STI framework and align it with European Union standards. The law encourages open access to scientific publications, supports integration with the European Research Area (ERA), and promotes transparency and data sharing. Development was led by MoES, with involvement from NASRI and the broader research community.

### B2.3 — Funding mechanisms and other policy instruments supporting open science

Two main instruments: (1) The **National Strategy for Scientific Research, Technology, and Innovation 2023–2030**, aligned with EU frameworks including Horizon Europe, includes initiatives to ensure scientific knowledge is shared openly. (2) **Law No. 109/2024** explicitly supports open science principles, managed by MoES and NASRI with multi-stakeholder involvement.

### B2.4 — Open science plan, strategy, or roadmap

The National Strategy for Scientific Research, Technology, and Innovation 2023–2030 serves as the primary roadmap. Key institutions involved include MoES (policy leadership), NASRI (research funding and implementation), the Academy of Sciences (knowledge dissemination), AIE (innovation ecosystem), and HEIs (education and research). The strategy emphasizes open access, data sharing, and scientific collaboration.

### B2.5 — Institutional open science policies

HEIs and research institutions are increasingly aligning with open science principles. HEIs have adopted policies requiring researchers to make their work publicly available in terms of data and findings. These institutional policies are largely shaped by and consistent with the national framework established through Law No. 109/2024 and the 2023–2030 Strategy.

### B2.6 — National funding mechanisms for open science

NASRI is the main body financing scientific research, managing public funds allocated to universities and research institutes. It requires open access and data sharing as conditions of grant funding. Additional funding comes through participation in Horizon Europe, which prioritizes open science principles.

### B2.7 — Integration of open science into public research funding criteria

NASRI requires that publicly funded research follow open access principles, including making publications and data available through online repositories. Researchers must ensure results of publicly funded projects are openly accessible. Grants actively encourage participation in open science platforms and collaborative, transparent research.

### B2.8 — Engagement of private sector in open science

NASRI funding calls encourage private-sector partnerships with research institutions in projects that are open and accessible. Research outcomes must be openly shared, ensuring that knowledge is not confined to one sector. The Technology Park facilitates collaboration between researchers, start-ups, and industry, promoting open-source technologies.

### B2.9 — National monitoring framework for open science

Albania does not yet have a monitoring framework specifically dedicated to open science. However, the National Strategy for Scientific Research, Technology, and Innovation 2023–2030 incorporates STI monitoring indicators that indirectly track open science progress. Plans are underway to develop more systematic monitoring mechanisms by the end of 2025.

## Section B3 – Open Science Infrastructure

### B3.1 — R&D expenditure as % of GDP

0.08% of GDP (data for 2022, published in 2024). Source: European Commission Albania Report 2024.

### B3.2 — Internet access rate

91.6% of the population had access to reliable internet in 2022 (data published 2023). Source: INSTAT Albania ICT Survey 2022.

### B3.3 — National Research and Education Network (NREN)

The main NREN is the **Albanian Academic Network (AAN / RASH)**. AAN provides specialized internet infrastructure and services tailored to the needs of research and education institutions (HEIs) across Albania. It connects universities and research institutes and facilitates participation in European research networks.

### B3.4 — Open science infrastructure (national)

MoES, in collaboration with NASRI and the National Information Society Agency (NAIS), is developing the **ACRIS platform** — a dynamic national database to promote open science and contain all national research products. The Center for Coordination and Dissemination of Scientific Information (QKPISH) at the Academy of Sciences coordinates **COBISS.AL** (online shared bibliographic system) and **ECRIS.AL** (information system tracking Albanian research activities), with a digital extension **dCOBISS** providing open access to scholarly articles.

### B3.5 — Open science infrastructure (international/regional)

Albania participates in the **Open Access Research Infrastructure in the Western Balkans Support Programme**, which supports the preparation of open access policies for regional research infrastructures. Albania is also a member of the **National Initiatives for Open Science in Europe (NI4OS-Europe)** project. Through this, Albania established the **Albanian Initiative for Open Science (AI4OS)**, a national coalition promoting open science policies in higher education and research.

### B3.6 — Collaboration on open science platforms and infrastructure

As an associated country in Horizon Europe, Albanian researchers collaborate with EU member state partners on open science platforms and infrastructure. Regional cooperation is fostered through the Western Balkans frameworks and the Regional Cooperation Council (RCC). The AI4OS initiative connects Albanian institutions with European open science networks for cross-border collaboration and knowledge sharing.

## Section B4 – Education, Capacity Building & Science Communication

### B4.1 — Capacity building initiatives on open science

Albania has both formal and informal capacity-building initiatives. Albanian universities, in collaboration with national and international partners, have organized workshops and seminars covering open access to research, open data, OER, and open science tools. Participation in Horizon Europe also contributes to capacity building, bringing European training and best practices. These are largely informal at this stage, with plans to formalize them in line with new regulatory frameworks under Law No. 109/2024.

### B4.2 — Capacity building for policymakers on open science

Law No. 109/2024 and the National Strategy for Scientific Research, Technology, and Innovation 2023–2030 include provisions for capacity-building programs for policymakers to integrate open science values into STI policies. These documents establish the foundational framework ensuring open science becomes a key pillar of policy development, promoting transparency, inclusivity, and international collaboration.

### B4.3 — Open science competencies in higher education curricula

A comprehensive framework for open science competencies has not yet been fully incorporated into higher education and research skills curricula. However, some HEIs include components on open data sharing, open access publishing, and other open science practices in their education and research programs. Integration is progressing gradually, encouraged by the national policy framework.

### B4.5 — Science communication initiatives

The Academy of Sciences of Albania (ASA) plays a central role in science communication. It publishes a wide range of scientific journals and periodicals across disciplines, available through COBISS.AL, ECRIS.AL, and dCOBISS — providing open access to scholarly articles. ASA also organizes conferences, workshops, and public events connecting researchers with the broader public.

## Section B5 – Research Assessment and Incentives

### B5.1 — Review of research assessment frameworks for alignment with open science

Discussions are underway to review and align existing research assessment and evaluation processes with open science values — particularly transparency, open data, and public access to scientific knowledge. The National Strategy for Science, Technology, and Innovation 2023–2030 emphasizes fostering open science practices within research and innovation, creating the policy basis for updating evaluation frameworks.

### B5.2 — Integration of open science in career evaluation and progression

The integration of open science practices (sharing, collaboration, societal engagement) into career evaluation systems is in progress but still at an early stage. Career evaluations in research institutions currently focus primarily on traditional indicators such as published papers, citations, and grant funding. Institutions and HEIs have begun emphasizing open science practices, but a full transition has not yet been made.

### B5.3 — Initiatives to recognize and reward open science practices

Efforts are being made to recognize and reward open science practices, with further development planned by end of 2025. Under Law No. 109/2024, a Council of Ministers' Decision is to be issued outlining the standards and criteria for recognizing open science contributions. This regulatory instrument will provide a clear framework for incentivizing and rewarding open science activities.

## Section B6 – Engagement of Societal Actors & Open Innovation

### B6.1 — Actions addressing societal engagement and open innovation

Albania addresses several recommended actions in this area: exploration of open peer-review practices; promoting open innovation practices; development of participatory strategies for identifying the needs of marginalized communities; and participatory methods that value inputs from societal actors, including citizen science and crowdsourcing.

QKPISH (part of the Academy of Sciences) has developed COBISS.AL and ECRIS.AL to support openness in the research cycle by providing public access to bibliographic records and data on the national research landscape. The dCOBISS digital repository further extends open access capabilities. These platforms support societal engagement with scientific knowledge.

## Section B7 – International Cooperation

### B7.1 — Promotion of international scientific collaboration on open science

MoES plays a key role in international collaboration through the National Strategy for Science, Technology, and Innovation 2023–2030, promoting open science in policy development and cross-border research. MoES and NASRI facilitate participation in Horizon Europe, which emphasizes open science principles. NASRI connects Albanian researchers with European and regional research networks and supports collaborative funding opportunities.

### B7.2 — Bilateral and regional cooperation on open science

Albania's EU integration process drives alignment of policies with EU standards, including Horizon Europe participation for cross-border research and innovation collaboration. Albania is a member of the **Regional Cooperation Council (RCC)**, which coordinates regional cooperation in science, technology, and innovation in the Western Balkans. Additional bilateral collaboration is conducted through the Western Balkans Open Access Research Infrastructure support programme.

### B7.3 — International funding mechanisms for open science

As an associated country in Horizon Europe, Albania benefits from and contributes to open access publishing requirements and collaborative open science projects. Western Balkans regional efforts are establishing regional funding mechanisms for joint research and innovation that incorporate open science principles. These mechanisms complement national funding managed by NASRI and aim to embed open science into multi-country research agendas.
