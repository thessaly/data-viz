### B1.2 — If yes, which language(s)?

### B1.3 — If yes, please provide details on the activities organized or foreseen and links as relevant

The RCN has incorporated open science values and principles in their assessment of grant applications  Link: Assessment of open science in grant applications Spescifically related to 16.d “Encouraging  multilingualism  in  the  practice  of  science,  in  scientific  publications and in academic communications” it is relevant to mention the Norwegian scheme for open journals in the social sciences and humanities (NÅHST) . The MRFF embeds open science policy in its grant opportunity guidelines, including requiring that grant recipients ensure that research publications are openly accessible and that de-identified research data following completion of the grant is in an open access repository and in accordance with best practice.

### B2.6 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so

No. We however have national negotiations for publish and read agreements for Norwegian institutions, coordinated by SIKT

### B2.7 — Please provide details and links as relevant

MRFF grantees are required to adhere to open science practices wherever possible, except for where this would present issues around privacy and confidentiality. Grantees are also strongly encouraged to publish de-identified research data and associated metadata in an open access repository or a public database and in accordance with best practice.
