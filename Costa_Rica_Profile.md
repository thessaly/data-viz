## B1 – Promotion of the UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science

### B1.1 — If yes, please indicate which ministries and or national authorities/entities have been involved in the promotion of the 2021 Recommendation

The National Council of Rectors (CONARE), conformed by the five public universities, has been developing activities since 2010, first to promote open access through repositories and journal portals and then, it has been involved in the promotion of open science, mainly regarding research data, the application of open indicators and the inclusion of preprints in some journals. The Ministry of Science, Innovation, Technology and Telecommunications (MICITT) and the National Council of Rectors (CONARE) held several workshops on Open Science in 2024, to promote knowledge among different sectors.

### B1.2 — If yes, which language(s)?

Spanish

### B1.3 — If yes, please provide details on the activities organized or foreseen and links as relevant

The Ministry of Science, Innovation, Technology and Telecommunications (MICITT) and the National Council of Rectors (CONARE) have held several workshops on Open Science in 2024 in order to sensitize the different sectors on UNESCO's Open Science recommendation. In addition, a working group was created between MICITT and CONARE to develop a National Open Science Strategy. Subsequently, in 2025 the working group was transformed into the Interinstitutional Commission for Open Science, formed by MICITT, CONARE and the Unit of Rectors of Private Universities (UNIRE). On the other hand, the National Council of Rectors (CONARE), conformed by the five public universities, has been developing activities since 2010, first to promote open access through repositories and journal portals and then, it has been involved in the promotion of open science, mainly regarding research data, the application of open indicators and the inclusion of preprints in some journals.

### B1.4 — If yes, please indicate the actions that are taken to incorporate the specific values and principles. For each case, please provide details and links as relevant

In 2024, the Ministry of Science, Innovation, Technology and Telecommunications (MICITT) and the National Council of Rectors (CONARE) created a technical working group to develop a National Open Science Strategy. Subsequently, in 2025 the working group was transformed into the Interinstitutional Commission for Open Science, formed by MICITT, CONARE and the Unit of Rectors of Private Universities (UNIRE).

## B4 – Education, Capacity Building & Science Communication

### B4.1 — Please indicate if those capacity building initiatives are formal or informal and provide more information and links as relevant

The National Council of Rectors (CONARE), conformed by the five public universities, has been developing activities since 2010, first to promote open access through repositories and journal portals and then, it has been involved in the promotion of open science, mainly regarding research data, the application of open indicators and the inclusion of preprints in some journals. At the higher education level, the National Council of Rectors (CONARE) created the Open Knowledge Subcommission to promote and disseminate open science.  The CONARE Network, created in 2009 by the National Council of Rectors (CONARE), in order to join the Latin American Cooperation of Advanced Networks, RedCLARA, and provide the national scientific community with the means to access and take advantage of advanced networks and scientific collaboration with the rest of the world. On the other hand, the Academic Network of the National University is a computer portal that becomes a search tool, which allows (among other functions) to display information about the academic staff of the National University, as well as the research programmes and projects in which they participate, publications and other information, in order to promote exchange and collaboration between peers from other latitudes in all disciplines of science.

### B4.2 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

The Open Science Declaration by the National Council of Rectors of Costa Rica (CONARE) seeks to promote the development of policies, strategies, capacity building, and collaborative actions necessary to encourage the implementation of Open Science strategies in public universities. Furthermore, it recommends to university communities that their scientific and scholarly output be published and shared in non-commercial Open Access (available in non-commercial repositories and publishers).

## B6 – Engagement of Societal Actors & Open Innovation

### B6.1 — Indicate which of the recommended actions they address

The Open Science Declaration by the National Council of Rectors of Costa Rica (CONARE) recommends the development of research evaluation models in public universities that involve open science indicators. It also encourages citizen participation in the different stages of the management of the research generated in public universities.  In addition, it seeks to promote the opening up of codes and systems that can be read and reproduced by others, so that knowledge can circulate and be distributed without physical, digital or language barriers.
