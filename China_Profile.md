## Key Indicators

**R&D expenditure (% GDP):** In 2024, China's total expenditure on Research and Experimental Development (R&D) surpassed ¥3.6 trillion, reaching ¥3.613 trillion, an increase of 8.3% compared to the previous year. The R&D expenditure intensity, defined as the ratio of R&D spending to Gross Domestic Product (GDP), rose to 2.68%, up by 0.10 percentage points from the previous year. Specifically, funding for basic research amounted to ¥249.7 billion, marking a 10.5% increase, and accounting for 6.91% of the total R&D expenditure, an improvement of 0.14 percentage points

**Internet access rate:** As of December 2024, China's internet users reached 1.108 billion, with an internet penetration rate of 78.6%. The number of online payment users was 1.029 billion, and online shopping users totaled 974 million. Both online retail sales and mobile payment penetration rates continue to lead globally. These statistics were released on January 17, 2025, in the 55th Statistical Report on China's Internet Development by the China Internet Network Information Centre (CNNIC). https://www.cac.gov.cn/2025-02/18/c_1741582058470997.htm

### B2.3 — Please provide details and links as relevant, including for each instrument: Title of the instrument, its objective, the authority in charge, allocated budget, elements of open science included

Yes, China has introduced specific policy tools to advance the development of open science. One significant initiative is the International Open Access Journals Recommended List (OARL), accessible at http://oarl.org.cn. Launched by China National Publications Import & Export (Group) Corporation in collaboration with Tsinghua University Library and the Society of China University Journals, the OARL serves as a guiding directory of international open access journals. By referencing international evaluation standards and leveraging global information resources, the OARL aims to provide Chinese researchers with authoritative recommendations for high-quality open access journals. The directory adheres to the fundamental principles of responsibility, transparency, dynamism, and collaboration, employing a review mechanism that combines expert qualitative assessments with quantitative metrics. This ensures that the selected journals meet high standards in publishing norms, quality control, and open practices, thereby offering valuable guidance to Chinese researchers in identifying and choosing reputable open access journals.

## B6 – Engagement of Societal Actors & Open Innovation

### B6.1 — Indicate which of the recommended actions they address

In the realm of scientific research, China has implemented several initiatives to promote open science across various stages:​ In the Research Infrastructure Sharing stage, the government issued the "Measures for the Open Sharing of National Major Scientific Research Infrastructure and Large Scientific Instruments," aiming to enhance the accessibility and utilization of significant research facilities and instruments. CAS Initiatives: The Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) established the "CAS Major Scientific and Technological Infrastructure Sharing Service Platform," facilitating the open sharing of its major scientific facilities. ​In the Open Access stage to Scientific Data, National bodies, along with institutions like CAS and the Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences, have formulated data management measures to promote data openness and utilization. In the Data Accessibility stage, centers such as the Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical Radio Telescope (FAST) have made their data publicly available to the global scientific community.​ In the Dissemination stage of Research Outcomes, organizations like CAS and the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) have issued statements advocating for open access, requiring that research papers be openly stored and accessible. In the repositorying stage of research outputs, CAS, in collaboration with universities, has developed institutional repositories (IRs), and the NSFC continues to build the Open Access Repository (OAR) platform to facilitate open access to research findings.​ In the stage of Open Education, higher education institutions are leveraging Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and other platforms to provide open educational resources, enhancing the accessibility of knowledge.​ In the International Scientific Cooperation, initiatives like the Digital Earth (DDE) and the Big Earth Data Center for Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) are engaging in global collaborations, serving as exemplary cases of open science practices.​

## B7 – International Cooperation

### B7.1 — How is your country promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science as outlined in the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science?

1)In line with the UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science, in order to promote global cooperation on ST&I, and work towards an open, fair, just and non-discriminatory environment for global science and technology development, and advance the sound development of global open science, the African Union, Brazil, China and South Africa put forward the Initiative on International Cooperation in Open Science in 2024 G20 Leaders’ Summit. 2) In alignment with the principles and implementation plans outlined in UNESCO's Recommendation on Open Science, the Alliance of National and International Science Organizations for the Belt and Road Regions (ANSO)—an international scientific organization based in Beijing, with the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) as a permanent member of its governing council—collaborated with CAS, UNESCO, and the African Academy of Sciences to establish the African "Open Science and Innovation Hub." This initiative is anchored in the China-Africa Joint Research Center, affiliated with CAS. For the Hub project, a budget allocation of $1 million annually has been earmarked for 2025. Several Global South countries and ANSO members will share their achievements and experiences in the realms of open science and innovation. Additionally, ANSO has budgeted $1.2 million for 2025 to support the joint research project "Open Science for Sustainable Development." The primary audience for this initiative comprises research institutions and scientists from countries along the "Belt and Road" route. Each project will be led by an ANSO member, collaborating with counterparts from other member countries to conduct interdisciplinary research. Furthermore, three "Open Science Training Courses" will be organized annually. For more information, please visit: http://www.anso.org.cn/. 3) To actively integrate China into global open science practices, enhance the open sharing of scientific literature and data, and foster openness, trust, and cooperation with the international scientific community, the China Association for Science and Technology (CAST) initiated the formation of the Open Science Promotion Consortium (OSPC). Currently, OSPC comprises numerous domestic and international research institutions, scientific organizations, publishing entities, information service providers, and other member units. Notably, several renowned overseas publishing houses, the International Association of Scientific, Technical, and Medical Publishers (STM), and prominent international academic groups are among its members.For more information, please visit: http://www.ospc.org.cn/. These initiatives underscore China's commitment to advancing open science and fostering international collaboration in scientific research and innovation.
