## B1 – Promotion of the UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science

### B1.1 — If yes, please indicate which ministries and or national authorities/entities have been involved in the promotion of the 2021 Recommendation

The 2021 Recommendation has been promoted as part of the development of the National Open Research Landscape Report (2021) and the National Action Plan for Open Research 2022 – 2030 (2022). The process of developing these reports was led by Ireland’s National Open Research Forum, a broad group that combines the expertise of representatives from policy, research funding and research performing organisations, the library sector, research infrastructures, enterprise and other key stakeholders in the research system across Ireland. The National Open Research Forum is a Government of Ireland initiative, funded by the Department of Further and Higher Education, Research, Innovation and Science, through the Higher Education Authority and supported by the Royal Irish Academy and the Digital Repository of Ireland. The National Action Plan for Open Research has been officially endorsed by 57 national and international entities, including national ministries, non-departmental state bodies, funders, institutions and affiliated organisations. The relevant ministries are the Department of Further and Higher Education, the Department of Agriculture, Food and Marine, the Department of the Environment, Climate and Communications, and the Open Data Initiative of the Department of Public Expenditure and Reform. Details of endorsing organisations are available here: https://norf.ie/national-action-plan/ Further details of the National Action Plan for Open Research are available in answer 2.2.

Aligned with A3.1.1 of the National Action Plan for Open Research, the NORF- funded Open Research Training Programme (TROPIC) aims to equip researchers at all career stages, including postgraduate students, with essential open research skills. The TROPIC project unites researchers and librarians from across Ireland's higher education institutions, spanning disciplines in science, engineering, social sciences, and the humanities. ## B5 – Research Assessment and Incentives

### B5.1 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

The National Action Plan for Open Research sets forth the overall national vision for establishing a culture of open research as follows: “By 2030 a culture of open research will be embedded at every level of the Irish research system. The reorientation of research recognition and rewards towards responsible research evaluation and open research metrics and practices will be well underway or complete, and approaches to research assessment will be broadly aligned at a system level across all stakeholders ... Embedding open research through responsible research evaluation and assessment, national training and education, and increased specialist support, will promote and support the quality and impact of Irish research”. Addressing A3.2.1 on the incentivisation of open research, A3.3.1 on research assessment and A3.3.2 on responsible research metrics of the National Action Plan for Open Research, the NORF-Funded Incentivising Open Research Practices in Ireland, locally known as ABROAD, brings together a strong consortium of research performing organisations, research funding organisations and key researcher groups to engage in deep, iterative dialogue with key stakeholders across the Irish research landscape. The project will use a consultation-driven approach to reach agreement on core commitments and concrete steps to effectively incentivise open research practices, develop a statement on the use of Responsible Research Metrics, and support the development of Responsible Research Assessment. ### B7.1 — How is your country promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science as outlined in the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science?

In line with A6.1.1 of the National Action Plan for Open Research, the National Open Research Coordinator represents NORF and presents on Open Research developments in Ireland nationally and internationally including through participation in the Council for Open Science Coordination (CoNOSC) and the Informal Commission Expert Group on Scientific Information. Irish institutions actively participate in the Research Data Alliance (RDA), which works to establish global, community-managed standards and infrastructures for research data sharing. The Digital Repository of Ireland (DRI) is the RDA-Ireland Node

### B7.2 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Interoperability, openness, and impact: Recommendations and roadmap for an Irish national PID strategy, presents 15 key recommendations and detailed actions aimed at establishing a more efficient, transparent, and open research ecosystem in Ireland, spanning the period until 2030. The objective is to enable seamless information flow between national and international systems and organisations, reduce bureaucratic burdens, and enhance research transparency and integrity. PIDs underpin this vision as they act as critical bridges between entities.
