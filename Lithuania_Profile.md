*UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science — Survey Responses*

## Key Indicators

**R&D expenditure (% GDP):** 1,03%  (2021)  (Source: State Data Agency (Statistics Lithuania)  https://osp.stat.gov.lt/moksliniai-tyrimai-ir-eksperimentine-pletra1)

**Internet access rate:** This year (2024), 90.4 % of households had Internet access at home. In urban areas, households having Internet access at home accounted for 92.7 %, in rural areas – 85.3 %. (Source: State Data Agency (Statistics Lithuania) https://vda.lrv.lt/en/news/internet-usage-continues-to-grow-over-90-of-lithuanian-households-have-its-access/)

## B1 – Promotion of the UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science

### B1.1 — If yes, please indicate which ministries and or national authorities/entities have been involved in the promotion of the 2021 Recommendation

Research Council of Lithuania

### B1.2 — If yes, which language(s)?

Lihuanian

### B1.4 — If yes, please indicate the actions that are taken to incorporate the specific values and principles. For each case, please provide details and links as relevant

The  article 51 of the Low on Research and Higher  Education was ammended in 2024 (https://e-seimas.lrs.lt/portal/legalAct/lt/TAD/TAIS.343430/asr). The article 51 covers Open Access, stipulating that publicly funded R&D results must be made publicly available in open access ways and means, insofar as this does not contradict the legislation regulating the protection of personal data, intellectual property, professional, commercial, or state and official secrets, as well as the protection of national security and defence and the protection of law-enforcement activities, and the activities of public security. ”

### B2.4 — Please provide details and links as relevant, including the name of ministries and national authorities/entities, as well as affiliated organizations that are involved in the development and implementation of the plan, strategy or the roadmap

The Research Council of Lithuania plans to develop an action plan for implementation of OA to the R&D Results  for harmonization of related procedures   by the end of 2026

### B2.5 — Please provide more information, including the number of policies, name of the institute(s) that has/have developed the policy(s) and elements of open science included in the policy (please consider policies and strategies addressing all elements of open science, including citizen
and participatory  science, or co-production of knowledge with multiple actors).

NHMRC’s Open Access Policy encourages research institutions to have or develop an institutional open science policy consistent with its Open Access Policy that addresses open access to both publications and data. NHMRC also encourages research institutions to consider rewarding open science practices as part of their recruitment, evaluation and promotion processes.

### B2.6 — Please provide details and links as relevant

Lithuanian Government provides financial support for participation in international research infrastructure (for membership fees and activities). The programme "Encourage Participation in International RIs", and Programme “Integration into EOSC” are under development. Open access expenses are eligible for R&D projects in Lithuania.

### B2.7 — Please provide details and links as relevant

Open Science practises are mandated (publications) or strongly encouraged (data) for all recipients of NHMRC funding. NHMRC grant funds may be used to support costs associated with publications and open access.

### B2.8 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so

This issue is under discussion but no concrete action has been agreed at this stage

### B2.9 — Please provide details and links as relevant

TThe Description of the monitoring procedure for open access to R&D results are planned to be elaborated by 2026. The monitoring areas are set in the Description of the Procedure for Open Access to the R&D Results

## B4 – Education, Capacity Building & Science Communication

### B4.1 — Please indicate if those capacity building initiatives are formal or informal and provide more information and links as relevant

Research and higher education institutions carry out studies in the field of ICT, as well as training data stewards for their own needs. The project "Integration into EOSC" is also foreseen to develop competences in the field of research data.

### B4.2 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so.

Due to the small size of the administration, no such training is provided.  Competences are developed through participation in Open Science related events

### B4.3 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Universities have special courses on Open Science for PHD student

### B4.5 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so.

Discussions are ongoing, but concrete solutions are not yet available due to resource constraints.

## B5 – Research Assessment and Incentives

### B5.1 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Institutions in the field of humanities earn extra points for scientifically structured datasets in annual evaluations.  The points earned for research outputs, including structured datasets, determine incentive R&D and arts funding for universities and research institutes.

### B5.2 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

The principles of Open Science are included in the list of desirable competences for researchers, approved by the Lithuanian Research Council  (source: https://www.e-tar.lt/portal/lt/legalAct/73b90eb0169511ee9f7ec2ffce8b47bc)

### B5.3 — For each initiative, please provide more information, including on the stakeholders[i] that are involved or lead the initiative, and links as relevant.

Vilnius University hosts the Open Science Awards event in September 2024 (source https://biblioteka.vu.lt/en/about/news/2318-for-the-first-time-at-vilnius-university-awards-for-significant-contribution-to-open-science)

## B6 – Engagement of Societal Actors & Open Innovation

### B6.1 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so.

Open Access provisions are being phased in gradually. Geopolitical changes require greater concern for security and autonomy. Open Access requirements are being introduced cautiously.

## B7 – International Cooperation

### B7.1 — How is your country promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science as outlined in the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science?

Lithuania plans to implement a programme for integration into the EOSC. The programme will upgrade scientific data repositories, build capacity, strengthen coordination of data management at national level and promote integration into EOSC

### B7.2 — If possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so.

Lithuania promotes internationalisation of research in the broadest sense (collaboration on open science is possible), but has not foreseen any real activities on open science due to limited resources

### B7.3 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so.

no such activities are currently planned
