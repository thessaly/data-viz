# Country Profile: Ecuador

*UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science — Survey Responses*

---

## Key Indicators

**R&D expenditure (% GDP):** In the case of Ecuador, the latest official data were collected in the National Survey of Science and Technology Activities (ACTI) (INEC - Senescyt, 2016) corresponding to 2014 and recording an investment in R&D with respect to GDP of 0.44%, a percentage that is below the minimum investment established in the COESCCI (2016) which is 0.55% of GDP.

**Internet access rate:** In the case of Ecuador, the latest official data was collected in the National Survey of Employment, Unemployment and Underemployment (ENEMDU) in 2024, which records that 66% of households in the country have access to the Internet.

### B2.2 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so

SENESCYT is the entity responsible for fulfilling commitment C15: "Co-creation of the National Open Science and Strategic Research Policy", framed in the Second Open Government Action Plan (PAGA), led by the Open Government Directorate of the Presidency of Ecuador. Until its closure, the document proposed for public policy was still in the development phase and aligned with the requirements established by the National Planning Secretariat for the issuance of public policies in the country. Therefore, the guidelines of the National Planning Secretariat have been followed, with the aim of obtaining a public policy resolution of national scope. The National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research is projected to be approved and issued in the second half of 2025. This policy will be aligned with UNESCO recommendations and international standards, promoting transparency, collaboration and equitable access to scientific knowledge.

### B2.4 — Please provide details and links as relevant, including the name of ministries and national authorities/entities, as well as affiliated organizations that are involved in the development and implementation of the plan, strategy or the roadmap

SENESCYT is in the process of formulating the National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research, whose approval and issuance is projected for the second half of 2025. Currently, the document is in the development phase and is aligned with the requirements established by the National Planning Secretariat for the issuance of public policies in the country. This process guarantees that the policy has a national scope, allowing its effective implementation in the science, technology and innovation system. In addition, it will be aligned with UNESCO recommendations and will promote the implementation of the values and principles of Open Science. For the issuance of the policy, the participation of the following entities is necessary: • Council for the Assurance of the Quality of Higher Education (CACES).- Public body that plans, coordinates and regulates the quality assurance system of higher education in Ecuador. • Higher Education Council (CES).- One of the bodies that govern the system and whose mission is the planning, regulation and internal coordination of the Higher Education System of Ecuador, and the relationship between its different actors with the Executive Branch and Ecuadorian society. • Higher Education System Assembly (ASESEC).- Body responsible for advising on the creation of an optimal higher education system. These entities play fundamental roles in the regulation and improvement of higher education. In particular, the CES, CACES and SENESCYT are the bodies in charge of regulating this sector. Therefore, the public policy proposal will be sent to the highest authority of these entities for review and validation of responsibilities, before the final issuance of the policy. This is essential, since the policy will affect all actors of the National System of Science, Technology, Innovation and Ancestral Knowledge, as well as the Higher Education System.

### B2.7 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so

The allocation of budgets by entities has not, to date, integrated open science practices into their strategies and guidelines for funding research. Therefore, the National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research aims to include guidelines and strategies aimed at developing funding mechanisms for research that incorporate open science practices. These mechanisms will be based on the budgets allocated to the entities and, when possible, will seek additional sources of resources.

### B2.8 — Please provide details and links as relevant

In the area of Open Science, SENESCYT has been a founding member and political authority of LA Referencia (Federated Network of Institutional Repositories of Scientific Publications) since 2010. This network supports national open access strategies in Latin America, through a platform with interoperability standards, which allows sharing and giving visibility to the scientific production generated by higher education and scientific research institutions. Since 2019, SENESCYT and the Ecuadorian Corporation for the Development of Research and Academia (CEDIA) have maintained a partnership through a technical-scientific cooperation agreement, which is renewed every two years, in order to continue supporting the operational management of LA Referencia. This collaboration has given rise to various national initiatives in the field of institutional repositories and open access, including the creation of a network of digital repositories, the metadata application project and training in repository standardization. Links: https://cedia.edu.ec/; https://rraae.cedia.edu.ec/vufind/ ; https://www.lareferencia.info/es/nodos/ecuador . Repositories of Higher Education Institutions. In Ecuador, the norm that requires universities to have a repository of theses was established in 2011, when the Higher Education Council (CES) approved the Technical Regulations for the management of degree projects. This regulation was part of an effort to regulate the academic degree process in the country's universities, promoting open access to research projects carried out by students. In addition, the Organic Law of Higher Education (LOES), which was approved in 2010, also establishes principles related to open access to academic and scientific information, strengthening transparency and collaboration in the educational field. Since then, universities in Ecuador are required to implement institutional repositories where theses, thesis works and other relevant academic documents are archived, ensuring that research results are publicly and digitally accessible, so they are non-commercial infrastructures and that in compliance with the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science, they comply with the FAIR and CARE principles. The Mintel open data portal. It is a platform for the exchange of knowledge from various areas available to citizens where research data is shared. SciELO Ecuador is an open access platform that is part of the SciELO (Scientific Electronic Library Online) network, a system that promotes free and open access to scientific and academic production in Latin America, the Caribbean, Spain and Portugal. The platform allows researchers, academics and students to access a wide range of scientific journals and academic articles from various areas of knowledge, such as social sciences, natural sciences, health, engineering, among others. Through SciELO Ecuador, we seek to promote free access and the dissemination of scientific knowledge, support the quality and visibility of Ecuadorian scientific publications and improve international collaboration between researchers from different countries. It also plays a key role in the development of open science, by offering users the possibility of accessing, reading and sharing works without economic or geographical barriers, making it a non-commercial platform that, in compliance with the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science, complies with the FAIR and CARE principles. ### B3.5 — Please provide links as relevant and more information, including the name of the infrastructure, date of establishment, language, target groups and beneficiaries, governance/community agreements, scope

Ecuador hosts the following information technology infrastructures for open science: - Ecuadorian Open Access Repository Network RRAAE The RRAAE Network (Ecuadorian Academic and Research Repository Network), managed by CEDIA (Ecuadorian Corporation for the Development of Research and Academia), was created in 2011 in Spanish. Until 2025, it has 58 participating institutions. The RRAAE is designed to interconnect the institutional repositories of universities and research centers in Ecuador, facilitating access and dissemination of scientific and academic production generated in the country. Its purpose is to promote open access, knowledge exchange and visibility of research work carried out in Ecuador, which is aligned with the principles of open science, which seek to make research and knowledge freely accessible. The RRAAE has a federated structure, meaning that it connects different repositories so that users can access a broad set of content from a single platform, regardless of which institution the research was generated at. This feature makes it similar to other federated infrastructures at an international level, such as the repositories of the SciELO Network or OpenAIRE in Europe. Although it is mainly focused on Ecuador, the RRAAE has the capacity to integrate with other repository and research networks at a regional and international level, allowing greater visibility and global access to Ecuadorian scientific production. This type of infrastructure facilitates access, exchange and collaboration in the field of scientific research at a national and international level. Link: https://rraae.cedia.edu.ec/vufind/ - SciELO-Ecuador Ecuador is part of the SciELO network since 2015. SciELO Ecuador is an open access portal that hosts the scientific publications of Ecuadorian journals. It is aligned with the principle of open science and its goal of making science accessible to all. SciELO Ecuador is part of an international network that connects repositories from various countries. This federation of portals allows Ecuadorian publications to be accessible at a regional and international level, promoting the exchange of knowledge and scientific collaborations. In addition, it facilitates interoperability with other scientific platforms and databases, allowing users to access research and publications from different countries within the same network. Link: http://scielo.senescyt.gob.ec/

## B4 – Education, Capacity Building & Science Communication

### B4.1 — Please indicate if those capacity building initiatives are formal or informal and provide more information and links as relevant

In Ecuador, there are initiatives carried out by members of civil society, such as the Openlab Ecuador Foundation, the institution's counterpart for the creation of the National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research. This entity has developed the Discovering Open Science Conference, an event that was born in Ecuador in 2020 in the midst of the COVID19 pandemic, and became a regional event with support, collaboration and contributions from the Ibero-American Open Science Community. Until 2024, four editions of this event have been held, which aims to spread open science to all possible corners and social actors. These conferences take place in several universities in Ecuador and are broadcast online for the entire region. They feature the participation of speakers, researchers and leaders of Open Science from Ibero-America. In addition, in 2024, the LATAM Open Science Forum was held in the city of Quito - Ecuador during the month of September 2024. This event brought together academics, researchers, students and activists interested in the challenges of Open Science in the region and the continent. In addition, keynote presentations, talks, workshops and networking spaces with the open science community were held. The objective of the event was: - Celebrate the achievements and recognize the most outstanding projects and initiatives in open science in Latin America. - Promote collaboration and the exchange of ideas between the Latin American and international scientific community. Create a space conducive to the exchange of ideas, the generation of work networks and the establishment of new strategic alliances. - Make visible the importance and highlight the crucial role of open science for the scientific, technological and social development of the region, in coherence with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG). - Promote the adoption of open science practices in universities and research institutions in the region. - Discuss the challenges and opportunities that open science presents for the scientific and technological development of Latin America. - Develop strategies to promote open science as a tool for inclusion, equity and social innovation. ### B5.1 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Within the framework of the formulation of the National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research, whose approval and issuance are planned for the second half of 2025, various actors participated in the working groups, including the Council for Quality Assurance of Higher Education (CACES) and the Council of Higher Education (CES). The CACES, as a public body in charge of planning, coordinating and regulating the quality assurance system in higher education in Ecuador, and the CES, responsible for the planning, regulation and coordination of the Higher Education System, agreed to incorporate criteria in the evaluation of the quality of scientific research, of teaching and research staff, and of higher education institutions. ## B7 – International Cooperation

### B7.1 — How is your country promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science as outlined in the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science?

Ecuador has made progress in promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science through various policies, initiatives and cooperation agreements. These efforts are aligned with the UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science (2021) and seek to guarantee open access to knowledge, strengthen scientific cooperation and encourage citizen participation in research. • Formulation of the National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research: Ecuador is in the process of developing its National Policy on Open Science and Strategic Research, whose approval and issuance are planned for the second half of 2025. This policy will establish guidelines for international collaboration, open access to data and research, and the integration of global standards in open science. • Participation in international networks and programs: Ecuador is part of regional and international organizations and consortia that promote collaboration in open science, such as the Federated Network of Institutional Repositories of Scientific Publications of Latin America (LA Referencia), which facilitates access to the country's scientific production; Ecuadorian universities and research centers have participated in Horizon Europe, the European Union program that funds research in open science and global cooperation; Cooperation agreements have been signed with international organizations, such as UNESCO and ECLAC, to strengthen open science and the transfer of knowledge. • Development of infrastructure for open access and scientific data: Ecuador has institutional digital repositories in universities and research institutions, promoting open access to scientific publications and data; The Digital Library of Ecuador (http://bibliotecasdelecuador.com/) guarantees the visibility of scientific knowledge produced in the country and its free access internationally. • Promotion of Citizen Science and the participation of social actors: Citizen science projects have been developed, such as the use of platforms such as iNaturalist and GBIF Ecuador for the collection of data on biodiversity; Educational programs and workshops have encouraged the participation of society in the generation and dissemination of scientific knowledge. • Incentives for scientific collaboration in open science: Evaluation criteria have been included in calls for funding for research that favor open access publication and international collaboration; Universities have promoted the adoption of open licenses in scientific publications and have facilitated the interoperability of their repositories with international systems.

### B7.2 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Ecuador has demonstrated a strong commitment to promoting open science and cross-border collaboration through various initiatives and strategies. Below are some of the most notable actions: 1. Co-creation of the National Open Science and Strategic Research Policy: Within the framework of the Second Open Government Action Plan (2022-2024), the Secretariat of Higher Education, Science, Technology and Innovation (SENESCYT) and the Openlab Ecuador Foundation committed to developing a national open science policy. This effort seeks to integrate the voices of multiple actors, including academia, the public sector and civil society, to foster inclusive and participatory governance in open science. 2. This participation facilitates cross-border collaboration and the exchange of best practices in open science, promoting interoperability and visibility of Ecuadorian scientific production at a regional level. 4. Organization of Events for the Dissemination of Open Science: Since 2020, Ecuador has hosted the "Jornadas Descubriendo la Ciencia Abierta", an annual event that seeks to disseminate the principles and practices of open science among various social actors. This space has had the collaboration of the Ibero-American open science community, strengthening international cooperation and the exchange of experiences. 5. Participation in International Forums: Ecuador has been present at events such as the LATAM Open Science Forum, co-organized by the Openlab Ecuador Foundation and the Ecuadorian Corporation for the Development of Research and Academia (CEDIA). These forums emphasize the relevance of open science as a driver of social change and promote new strategic alliances at the regional level. These initiatives reflect Ecuador's commitment to promoting open science and cross-border collaboration, aligning with international recommendations to foster more inclusive and accessible science.
