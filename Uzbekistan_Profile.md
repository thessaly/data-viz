## B1: Promotion of the UNESCO 2021 Recommendation

### B1.1: Promotion to ministries and national authorities
Yes, the 2021 Recommendation has been promoted and shared with appropriate ministries, institutions, and affiliated organizations in our country through the following channels:
Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovations – This ministry is responsible for policy development and coordination of scientific and innovation-related initiatives. It has played a key role in sharing and promoting the Open Science Recommendation as part of its ongoing efforts to foster scientific progress.
Agency for Innovative Development – Actively involved in implementing and promoting innovative strategies and policies, the Agency has incorporated aspects of Open Science into its projects and initiatives, contributing to national awareness and implementation.
Center for Scientific and Technical Information – This center plays a pivotal role in the dissemination of scientific and technical advancements. It has supported awareness-building campaigns and educational programs that highlight the importance of Open Science and its benefits.
Other Affiliated Research Institutions and Universities – Various research institutions and universities have engaged in discussions, workshops, and training sessions aimed at integrating the Open Science principles outlined in the 2021 Recommendation into national strategies. These institutions have actively worked to promote the widespread adoption of Open Science practices across their communities.

### B1.2: Translation into national language(s)
No, the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science is not fully available in all national languages of our country at this moment. However, a Russian version of the document is currently available. There are also plans to translate it into Uzbek in the near future.

### B1.3: Awareness-raising activities organised
Yes, several awareness-raising activities on Open Science, including its key elements, associated benefits, and challenges, have been organized or are planned to be organized by national authorities and entities by the end of 2025. These activities include:
Workshops and Training Sessions – Organized by the Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovations, along with the Agency for Innovative Development, these sessions aim to educate researchers, academics, and policymakers about Open Science principles, its benefits, and strategies for implementation.
National Conferences and Forums – These events are designed to foster discussions among scientists, universities, and research institutions on Open Science practices, challenges, and opportunities, helping to build a collaborative approach toward Open Science.
Publications and Online Resources – Informational materials and digital platforms are being developed to raise awareness of Open Science among a broader audience, including resources that highlight its importance and the practical steps involved in its adoption.
Collaboration with International Organizations – Engagement in partnerships with UNESCO and other international bodies to align national Open Science policies with global best practices, ensuring that the country remains connected to ongoing international efforts in promoting Open Science.

## B2: Open Science Policies & Governance

### B2.1: National STI policy with open science elements
Yes.
Title of the policy: Decree of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. PF-165 "On approval of the Strategy for the Innovative Development of the Republic of Uzbekistan for 2022 — 2026."

The authority in charge of policy development: Ministry of Higher Education, Science, and Innovations of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
Entities involved: Ministry and administrative bodies, the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Karakalpakstan, regional governments, and the Tashkent city government.
Year of adoption: July 6, 2022.
Key areas/sections of the policy: The strategy prioritizes the following sectors in its implementation:
Supporting startup initiatives and organizing large-scale production by forming a network of innovative infrastructure entities, such as innovative technology parks, technology transfer centers, innovation clusters, venture organizations, innovation centers, startup accelerators, and incubators.
Increasing the share of innovatively active organizations by improving institutional mechanisms for state support of innovative activities.
Ensuring rapid socio-economic growth of regions by increasing the innovative activity of small businesses.
Stimulating demand for innovation by establishing a comprehensive system for creating new types of products and innovative technologies, from the initial idea to the final consumer.
Creating a system for redirecting capital towards "radically renewing" innovations.
Further developing human capital in the management of innovative activities by fostering creativity, innovative entrepreneurship, and rationalization skills at all stages of education.

## B3: Infrastructure

### B3.1: R&D expenditure as % of GDP
In accordance with Appendix 2 of the Decree of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. PF-6097, dated October 29, 2020, the task of gradually increasing the funds allocated from the state budget to the science sector to 2 percent of gross domestic product (GDP) by 2030, including 0.8 percent of GDP by 2022, has been set.
Organizational measures have been taken to implement the task, including the diversification of science and technology, as outlined in Resolution No. PP-307 'On Organizational Measures to Implement the Strategy of Innovative Development of the Republic of Uzbekistan for 2022-2026.' In 2022, a total of 5 trillion 853 billion 974 million soums were allocated to science, which accounted for 0.8 percent of gross domestic product (GDP). This included 1 trillion 102 billion 9 million soums from the state budget, 2 trillion 537 billion 750 million soums from organizations and industrial enterprises with a state share, 2 trillion 11 billion 474 million soums from the private sector, and 201.85 billion soums from higher education and scientific organizations. This indicator is expected to reach 0.4 percent of GDP in 2024.
Also, in accordance with paragraph 10 of Appendix 2 to the Decree of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. PF-165, dated July 6, 2022, it is planned to increase total spending on research and development to 0.19% of GDP by 2024. According to the Statistics Agency under the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the forecast for the volume of gross domestic product in Uzbekistan for 2024 has increased by 6.6% in current prices, reaching 1.14 quadrillion soums.
https://stat.uz/uz/matbuot-markazi/qo-mita-yangiliklar/58374-2024-yilning-yanvar-sentabr-oylarida-yalpi-ichki-mahsulot-yaim-ning-o-sish-sur-atlari
In accordance with paragraph 18 of Appendix 3 to the Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan 'On the State Budget of the Republic of Uzbekistan for 2023,' based on primary data, 778 billion 200 million soums were allocated from the State Budget for research and development this year. Of this, 693.5 billion soums were allocated for financing projects within the framework of state programs on scientific activity, and 84.7 billion soums were allocated for the organization and additional equipment of scientific laboratories with new, modern high-tech equipment.
Additionally, this year, total expenditures on research and development carried out by the private sector amounted to 3 trillion 803 billion 416 million soums, bringing the total gross expenditures for research and development to 4 trillion 581 billion 616 million soums.
Taking the above into account, the gross expenses for research and development this year amounted to 0.40 percent of the forecasted GDP for 2024 in the republic.

### B3.2: Population with access to reliable internet
As of early 2024, Uzbekistan's internet penetration rate stood at 83.3%, indicating that approximately 83.3% of the country's population had access to reliable internet services. 
In terms of internet connection speeds, as of January 2024, the median fixed-line broadband download speed was 59.07 megabits per second (Mbps), while the median mobile broadband download speed was 26.97 Mbps. 
These figures suggest that a significant portion of Uzbekistan's population has access to reliable internet services, with varying connection speeds depending on the type of service.

## B4: Education, Capacity Building & Science Communication

### B4.1: Systematic capacity building on open science
Systematic capacity building on open science has taken place in Uzbekistan’s higher education and research-performing institutions.
This includes training programs, workshops, and seminars organized by the Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovations, Agency for Innovative Development, and universities. These initiatives aim to educate researchers and students on open science principles, data sharing, and collaborative research practices.

### B4.2: Capacity building for policymakers
Uzbekistan has undertaken capacity-building programs for policymakers to integrate open science principles into Science, Technology, and Innovation (STI) policies and strategies. For example, the National Training Workshop on Science, Technology, and Innovation (STI) Park Policies and Practices for Sustainable Development aimed to build the capacity of Uzbekistan’s policymakers and STI park practitioners in designing and implementing STI park policies and managing STI parks. 
Additionally, the Modernizing Uzbekistan’s National Innovation System Project (MUNIS), supported by the World Bank, focuses on enhancing the capacity of public research organizations to achieve international standards of research quality and enable the commercialization of research results.

### B4.3: Open science competencies in higher education curricula
There have been efforts to incorporate open science competencies into higher education research skills curricula in Uzbekistan.
Educational institutions, such as universities and research centers, have begun to integrate open science principles into their programs. This includes training on data sharing, open-access publishing, and collaborative research. Additionally, it is expected that by 2025, more comprehensive frameworks for open science competencies will be fully integrated into curricula across universities, driven by initiatives from the Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovations and related bodies.

### B4.5: Science communication initiatives
Universities and research institutes in Uzbekistan have been granted two years of free access to international scientific databases. Consulting and technical assistance for organizing access is provided by the Center for Scientific and Technical Information. Currently, there are four national subscriptions to international databases in Uzbekistan:
Web of Science: 224 organizations have received access for 3 years.
Springer Nature: 210 organizations have received access for 3 years.
Pivot-RP: 224 organizations have received access for 2 years.
On an ongoing basis, events are held across different regions of the republic to disseminate information about the work and capabilities of these international scientific databases. In 2024 alone, 57 webinars, 51 seminars, and 2 forums were held in various regions and cities of the republic with the participation of foreign specialists

### B5.3: Initiatives to recognise/reward open science practices
Uzbekistan has undertaken initiatives to recognize and reward open science practices, with plans to expand these efforts by the end of 2025.
The Concept of Development of Science until 2030 outlines strategies to enhance the scientific sector, including the introduction of a national rating system to evaluate the effectiveness of scientific and innovative activities. This system aims to improve social partnerships between the state and scientific organizations, fostering a culture of open science. 
Additionally, the government plans to increase the share of private sector funding for research and development from 8% to 20% by 2025, and to 30% by 2030. This initiative is expected to incentivize open science practices by providing financial support and recognition to innovative projects. 
These measures reflect Uzbekistan's commitment to promoting open science and rewarding practices that align with its values and principles.

## B7: International Cooperation

### B7.1: Promoting international scientific collaborations on open science
Uzbekistan is actively promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science, aligning with the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science. Key initiatives include:
Bilateral Agreements: In October 2023, Uzbekistan and China signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to establish an Open Science Framework. This agreement aims to enhance interoperability between the two countries' scientific data platforms, facilitating the open sharing of scientific data and fostering collaborative research efforts. 
Engagement with UNESCO: Uzbekistan is actively involved in UNESCO's Open Science initiatives, contributing to the development of international standards and frameworks that promote open science practices globally. This engagement underscores Uzbekistan's commitment to fostering international cooperation in open science. 
Participation in International Platforms: Uzbekistan is a participant in the UNESCO Open Science Platform, which supports the implementation of the 2021 Recommendation. This platform provides a collaborative tool for scientists, communities, policymakers, and the public, facilitating open access to scientific knowledge and promoting cross-regional collaboration.

### B7.3: Regional/international funding mechanisms for open science
Uzbekistan is actively involved in discussions and initiatives aimed at establishing regional and international funding mechanisms for open science. Key efforts include:
Engagement with UNESCO: Uzbekistan participates in UNESCO's initiatives to promote open science, which includes discussions on establishing funding mechanisms to support open science efforts globally. This involvement aligns with the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science, which encourages countries to maximize the impact of public funding and establish regional and international funding mechanisms for promoting and strengthening open science. 
Collaboration with the Economic Cooperation Organization (ECO): Uzbekistan is a member of the ECO Science Foundation, an intergovernmental organization that aims to promote scientific and technological research among its member states, including Uzbekistan. The foundation undertakes various initiatives to foster scientific excellence and technical expertise within its member countries, which may include discussions on funding mechanisms for open science. 
National Initiatives: At the national level, Uzbekistan has undertaken initiatives to enhance its science and technology infrastructure, which may involve discussions on funding mechanisms for open science. For example, the "Science, Technology and Innovation Potential of the Regions of the Republic of Uzbekistan" document outlines plans to study the financing of scientific research and mechanisms for the commercialization of scientific research results.
