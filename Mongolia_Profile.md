### B1.4 — If yes, please indicate the actions that are taken to incorporate the specific values and principles. For each case, please provide details and links as relevant

Specific actions have been undertaken and are planned to be implemented in Mongolia by the end of 2025 to incorporate the values and guiding principles of open science into publicly funded research, in alignment with the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation (sections 16.a–16.e, 16.h). The Ministry of Economy and Development, together with the Mongolian Academy of Sciences (MAS), has initiated a review and revision of national science and innovation policies to reflect key open science principles such as quality, integrity, equity, transparency, and inclusiveness. These values are expected to be embedded in the forthcoming National Science, Technology, and Innovation Roadmap (2025–2035). At the institutional level, universities and public research organizations—such as the National University of Mongolia, the University of Finance and Economics (UFE), and the Information Technology Center of MAS—have developed and are maintaining institutional repositories to facilitate open access to scientific publications and research data. These platforms enhance transparency, data reproducibility, and collective benefit by making the results of publicly funded research openly available to the broader community. Moreover, national research funding mechanisms are increasingly designed to promote inclusivity and equitable access. Funding calls now prioritize the participation of young researchers, women scientists, and institutions in rural or less-resourced areas, and encourage collaboration between public and private sectors. Efforts are also being made to integrate traditional knowledge systems into research projects, reinforcing values of diversity and respect. Looking ahead, Mongolia is working toward the establishment of a national open science framework that will provide formal guidelines on responsible data management, open access, open-source software use, and research transparency. This framework aims to institutionalize open science practices across all stages of the research cycle, ensuring flexibility, accountability, and sustainability in publicly funded research.

## B2 – Open Science Policies and Governance

### B2.1 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so

Mongolia does not currently have a formal, comprehensive national policy, strategy, or plan of action specifically dedicated to science, technology, and innovation (STI). However, the government and relevant ministries are actively working toward developing such a framework to better coordinate and support scientific research, technology development, and innovation across the country. Efforts are underway to consult stakeholders and align future policy initiatives with international standards and recommendations, including the principles outlined in the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science.

### B2.2 — Please share a machine-readable PDF file(s) of the policy(ies) and provide the following information for each: Title of the policy or legal instrument, the authority in charge of development of the policy, entities involved and the process of development, year of adoption, year of last update, key areas/sections of the policy, elements of open science that are addressed, and links to the webpage(s) if available

Mongolia has a national legal framework that addresses open science and its key elements in alignment with the values and principles outlined in the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science. Specifically, the Law on Science and Technology, which was renewed in 2024, provides the foundation for governing science, technology, and innovation activities in the country. This law incorporates provisions promoting transparency, open access to scientific knowledge, data sharing, and collaboration among research institutions. It reflects the core values of open science such as quality, integrity, inclusiveness, and collective benefit, and sets guiding principles for the ethical and responsible conduct of publicly funded research. The updated legal framework aims to support the development of an enabling environment for open science practices across Mongolia. Unfortunately, it has not translated into English.

Mongolia has specific policy instruments aimed at promoting open science, primarily through administrative acts issued by relevant governmental bodies. They focus on supporting open access to scientific publications and data, encouraging collaboration among research institutions, and ensuring transparency and accountability in publicly funded research. These instruments outline clear objectives, target beneficiaries, resources, and indicators to deliver tangible products and long-term impacts in advancing open science practices in Mongolia.

In Mongolia, various research-performing institutions, universities, and scientific organizations are promoting open science in line with the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation. At the institutional level, universities such as the National University of Mongolia and the University of Finance and Economics, along with the Mongolian Academy of Sciences, have begun to implement internal strategies to encourage open access to research, data sharing, and broader collaboration.  In addition, the Ministry of Economy and Development plays an active role in promoting open science by financing access to global open science resources and platforms. The Ministry currently covers subscription and access fees for major international databases and open-source platforms such as Springer Nature, EBSCO, and Dimensions. This support ensures that Mongolian researchers and students have equitable access to high-quality scientific knowledge, thereby reinforcing the values of inclusiveness, equity, and collective benefit outlined in the 2021 Recommendation. These efforts contribute significantly to building an enabling environment for open science across research institutions in Mongolia.

### B2.6 — Please provide details and links as relevant

While Mongolia does not yet have funding mechanisms specifically and exclusively dedicated to open science, the country does provide research grants through national funding programs that support publicly funded research. These grants, administered primarily through the Ministry of Education and Science and the Mongolian Foundation for Science and Technology, can be used to support activities that align with open science practices—such as open access publishing, data sharing, and collaborative research.  Although not explicitly framed as “open science” funding, these mechanisms contribute indirectly to the advancement of open science by enabling research institutions and individual researchers to produce and disseminate scientific knowledge. The government also supports infrastructure development and capacity-building initiatives which can include the development of institutional repositories and research information systems.  Expanding these mechanisms and integrating open science as an explicit funding objective remains a potential area for future policy development in Mongolia, in line with the principles outlined in the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science.

### B2.7 — Please provide details and links as relevant

In Mongolia, open science practices have been increasingly integrated into existing research funding mechanisms. National research grants—particularly those managed by the Ministry of Economy and Development and the Mongolian Foundation for Science and Technology—support activities that promote open access to scientific publications, research data sharing, and collaboration among researchers. These mechanisms also encourage engagement with societal stakeholders, including sectors such as health, environment, and agriculture, aligning with the principles of inclusiveness and collective benefit. In addition, Mongolia has initiated the development of a regulation for the establishment of an intersectional laboratory, which will serve as a shared platform accessible to researchers across various institutions and disciplines. This initiative is designed to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, promote the use of shared scientific infrastructure, and enhance the accessibility of research tools and data. It represents a concrete step toward enabling open science practices in line with the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation, particularly with respect to open infrastructure, researcher collaboration, and public engagement in science.

### B2.8 — Please provide details and links as relevant

Mongolia is actively working to foster equitable public-private partnerships (PPPs) in open science by the end of 2025, in alignment with the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science. A significant development is the enactment of the Public-Private Partnership Law in December 2023. This law establishes a clear legal framework for implementing major infrastructure and public service projects through private investment on mutually beneficial terms. It aims to enhance coordination between public institutions and private entities, facilitating collaborative efforts in various sectors, including science and technology .  Additionally, the Ministry of Economy and Development, in cooperation with the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD), organized a national training on "Policies and Experiences of Science, Technology, and Innovation Parks" in April 2025. The training aimed to strengthen the foundation of development and the economy based on science, technology, and innovation in Mongolia. It brought together representatives of science and technology parks, business incubators, co-working offices, technology transfer centers, and startup entrepreneurs, who had the opportunity to meet and exchange experiences with delegates from countries that are successfully developing such park .  Furthermore, the Mongolian National Chamber of Commerce and Industry (MNCCI) and the Science Park are collaborating to establish a working group, draft a cooperation agreement, and create a detailed plan. Their aim is to facilitate the exchange of scientific information and research findings between scientific organizations and businesses, thereby promoting innovation and technology transfer.  These initiatives demonstrate Mongolia's commitment to developing a robust ecosystem for open science through equitable public-private partnerships, ensuring that scientific knowledge and innovations are accessible and beneficial to all sectors of society.

### B3.3 — Please provide more information and links as relevant, and indicate to which open science actors in your country those networks are accessible

Mongolia has an active National Research and Education Network (NREN) known as ErdemNet. Established in 2000, ErdemNet was created to enhance communication, information exchange, and research access within the fields of education, culture, and science across the country. In 2012, the network expanded with the establishment of an educational data center, supported by joint funding from the state budget and the Asian Development Bank. This expansion involved the procurement and installation of servers, equipment, and network resources to strengthen the infrastructure of the education sector's information system. By 2014, ErdemNet had taken over the internet services for schools and education departments in provincial centers, further solidifying its role as the backbone of Mongolia's NREN .  ErdemNet's primary mission is to support the needs of the research and education communities in Mongolia by providing specialized internet services tailored to their requirements. This includes facilitating access to digital learning platforms, research databases, and collaborative tools essential for academic and scientific endeavors. In addition to ErdemNet, Mongolia has partnered with Eduten, a Finnish digital math learning platform developed by the University of Turku. In 2023, the Ministry of Education and Science of Mongolia signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Eduten to introduce this platform into Mongolian schools. The Eduten platform offers a library of over 200,000 pedagogically high-quality tasks for students aged 6 to 18, aiming to improve mathematics education in the country .  These initiatives demonstrate Mongolia's commitment to enhancing its research and education infrastructure through the development and expansion of national networks and international collaborations.

## B4 – Education, Capacity Building & Science Communication

### B4.1 — Please indicate if those capacity building initiatives are formal or informal and provide more information and links as relevant

Mongolia has initiated the establishment of a new university under the Mongolian Academy of Sciences (MAS). On May 15, 2024, the Mongolian Cabinet approved a resolution to develop science and technology, which includes the founding of a university leveraging the MAS's existing infrastructure and human resources. The goal is to prepare specialized human resources in leading fields of science and technology, strengthen national capabilities, and intensify interdisciplinary research and development. The university will offer professional courses and programs in collaboration with relevant universities, focusing on technology transfer and the application of academic research into production and services . This initiative aims to enhance the integration of research and education, fostering innovation and the adoption of advanced technologies in Mongolia. The university is expected to play a pivotal role in developing a skilled workforce capable of addressing the country's scientific and technological challenges.

### B4.2 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Not yet systematically. In Mongolia, while general capacity-building efforts related to science, technology, and innovation (STI) have been implemented, there have not yet been formal or comprehensive training programmes specifically targeting policymakers on how to integrate the core values and principles of open science into STI policies and strategies.  However, awareness is gradually increasing. Some indirect efforts have occurred through international cooperation, workshops, and participation in regional forums related to open science and digital transformation. These engagements have exposed policymakers to global best practices and the importance of openness, transparency, inclusivity, and collaboration in science policy. With the recent adoption of updated laws and strategic initiatives, such as the establishment of a university under the Mongolian Academy of Sciences and the modernization of digital platforms like E-Mongolia, there is growing momentum that could lead to more targeted capacity-building programs in the near future.

### B4.3 — Please, if possible, briefly explain the reason and the difficulties or obstacles in this area and indicate if there are plans to do so.

As of now, Mongolia has not yet systematically incorporated a comprehensive framework of open science competencies into higher education research curricula. However, there are ongoing initiatives that lay the groundwork for such integration. The Mongolian University of Science and Technology (MUST) has been a pioneer in promoting open education and digital learning. In 2019, MUST established the Open Education Center (OEC), focusing on developing Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs), Open Educational Resources (OER), and blended learning models. The OEC has introduced training modules on topics such as Open Education, Instructional Design, and MOOCs, which indirectly support the development of open science competencies among educators and researchers.  Additionally, in 2015, UNESCO and MUST launched an online course titled “New Technologies in Effective Teaching and Learning in Higher Education in the Context of Mongolia.” This course, available in Mongolian, includes modules on Open Educational Resources, open licensing, and mobile learning, providing foundational knowledge that aligns with open science principles.  While these initiatives are steps in the right direction, a fully integrated framework of open science competencies within higher education curricula has not been formally established. Looking ahead, the Education Sector Medium-Term Development Plan (2021–2030) envisions the transition of Mongolia’s education system into a digital framework by 2030, which may include the development of such competencies.  Therefore, while there is no formal framework in place yet, the foundational elements are being developed, and it is possible that a comprehensive integration of open science competencies into higher education curricula could be realized by the end of 2025.

## B5 – Research Assessment and Incentives

### B5.1 — Please provide more information and links as relevant

Mongolia has initiated efforts to review and align research assessment and evaluation processes with open science principles, with further developments anticipated by the end of 2025. In April 2025, the Government of Mongolia approved new regulations—“Evaluation Procedures,” “Monitoring Procedures,” and “Evaluation and Review of Organizational Activities and Results”—under Government Resolution No. 43. These regulations aim to enhance the coherence of development policies, ensure regular measurement of implementation processes, and evaluate results based on research and analysis. The implementation of these procedures is expected to improve policy implementation, resource allocation, and accountability, aligning with open science values of transparency and evidence-based decision-making .  Additionally, in November 2024, the Mongolian Academy of Sciences (MAS) co-hosted an international training workshop on open science infrastructures for disaster management. This workshop focused on building resilient open science infrastructures to support disaster management, emphasizing the importance of open data and collaborative research practices.

### B5.3 — For each initiative, please provide more information, including on the stakeholders[i] that are involved or lead the initiative, and links as relevant.

Mongolia has initiated several measures to recognize and reward open science practices, with further developments anticipated by the end of 2025. In 2024, the 8th National Conference of Mongolian Young Scientists focused on "Open Science – Youth Participation," highlighting the importance of open science principles. The conference included the Khureltogoot Academic Conference, where over 500 research submissions were reviewed, and 28 studies received grants. Notably, the grant amount for young post-doctoral researchers was increased from 4 million MNT to 20 million MNT, reflecting a commitment to supporting open science initiatives . Additionally, the Innovation 7 Days event organized the Best Paper Awards, with a prize fund of 30 million MNT, recognizing outstanding research contributions. These awards provide incentives for researchers to engage in open science practices and disseminate their findings . Furthermore, the Russian Science Foundation and the Ministry of Education and Science of Mongolia (2024) signed a partnership agreement to support joint research projects. This collaboration encourages open sharing of research outcomes and fosters international cooperation, aligning with open science principles .
