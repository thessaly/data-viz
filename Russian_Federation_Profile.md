Title of the policy or legal instrument   Concept of International Scientific and Technical Cooperation of the Russian Federation the authority in charge of development of the policy   The Presidential Council for Science and Education entities involved and the process of development     The Federal Assembly of the Russian Federation shall ensure the development of the regulatory framework for the International Scientific and Technical Cooperation. The development of strategic, planning and program documents to ensure the implementation of the Concept is assigned to the Ministry of Science and Higher Education of the Russian Federation. At the international, national, regional and municipal levels, individual functional areas and tasks related to the implementation of the International Scientific and Technical Cooperation, are ensured by: - Federal Agency for the Commonwealth of Independent States, Compatriots Living Abroad, and International Humanitarian Cooperation (Rossotrudnichestvo), including its offices abroad; - The Ministry of Science and Higher Education of the Russian Federation; - The Ministry of Economic Development of the Russian Federation; - The Ministry of Industry and Trade of the Russian Federation; - the Russian Academy of Sciences; - other federal executive bodies and state corporations; - Russian development institutes and representative offices of companies with state participation abroad; - leading organizations carrying out research and development, federal and national research universities and other leading universities. At the regional level, the implementation of the provisions of the Concept is the prerogative of regional authorities responsible for scientific and technical/educational activities. year of adoption   2019 year of last update   It is planned to update the Concept in 2025 key areas/sections of the policy  – development of a system for training domestic and attracting the best world personnel, development of competencies in the field of scientific research, development and innovation, as well as international scientific and technical cooperation and science diplomacy; - formation in Russia of a comfortable environment for scientific research, development and innovation within the framework of International Scientific and Technical Cooperation, including through the improvement of institutions, norms, support mechanisms and forms of organization of scientific and technical activities and International Scientific and Technical Cooperation; - development of modern research and technological (including digital) infrastructure; - creation of new forms of international scientific and technical cooperation, development of science diplomacy; - ensuring within the framework of International Scientific and Technical Cooperation effective transfer and commercialization of the results of scientific and technical activities in the Russian and global economy; - formation of international regimes and standards, institutions and processes that ensure the effective implementation of the International Scientific and Technical Cooperation of the Russian Federation. elements of open science that are addressed  The international scientific and technical cooperation of the Russian Federation is carried out on the principle of openness. Openness implies - with reasonable measures to strengthen the protection of intellectual property and other results of intellectual activity: - freedom of scientific and technological communication; - free, equal and non-discriminatory access to scientific information, research results, scientific infrastructure, competencies, as well as individual technological developments that are important for solving humanitarian and other universal problems. and links to the webpage(s) if available https://minobrnauki.gov.ru/upload/2021/07/kontseptsiya_MNTS_Rossiyskoy_Federatsii.pdf

### B3.5 — Please provide links as relevant and more information, including the name of the infrastructure, date of establishment, language, target groups and beneficiaries, governance/community agreements, scope

Title of the infrastructure   the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) Date of establishment   March 26, 1956 Language   Russian and English Governance/community agreements JINR is a legal entity and possesses on the territory of the member states the legal capacity necessary for the realization of its functions. In accordance with its status as an intergovernmental organization, JINR has international legal personality, in particular, it has the right to conclude international agreements and to have observers at other international organizations, the activities of which correspond to the statutory objectives of JINR. Scope: •	research areas Particle physics, nuclear physics and condensed matter physics •	elements of open science - JINR Laboratories; - scientific research base (experimental facilities for physics, powerful high-productive computing environment); - international cooperation; - educational activities; - open access publications. •	stage(s) of research - conducting theoretical and experimental research in the accepted scientific directions of its activity; - exchange of experience in conducting scientific research, as well as information obtained as a result of this research, by publishing scientific papers, holding conferences, symposiums, organizing internships; - establishing and maintaining contacts with other national and international scientific institutions and organizations in order to coordinate activities and organize mutually beneficial cooperation. •	accessibility to all the relevant users in the region or internationally Participation in JINR activities is implemented in diverse forms: on the basis of membership in JINR, as well as bilateral and multilateral agreements on participation in the implementation of individual scientific programs. On the basis of agreements concluded with JINR, international and national research organizations and institutions, as well as individual scientists from non-member states may participate in JINR's scientific projects. In order to exchange information, establish cooperation and coordinate activities, international organizations might be granted observer status with the JINR. and links to the webpage if available   https://www.jinr.ru/about-en/

Title of initiatives or legal instrument   the Decade of Science and Technology in the Russian Federation the authority in charge of development of the policy   the President of the Russian Federation key areas/sections of the policy - attracting talented young people to the area of research and development; - promoting the involvement of researchers and developers in solving the most significant social and national development issues; - increasing accessibility of information on the achievements and prospects of Russian science for citizens of the Russian Federation. year of adoption   2022 entities involved and the process of development The Government of the Russian Federation approves the plan for the Decade of Science and Technology in the Russian Federation. Federal executive bodies ensure the implementation of the plan’s activities and develop departmental plans of the main activities for the Decade of Science and Technology in the Russian Federation. Regional government bodies are involved in the implementation of the Decade of Science and Technology in the Russian Federation and develop regional plans of the main activities for the implementation of the Decade of Science and Technology in the Russian Federation. The plan for the Decade of Science and Technology in the Russian Federation contains information on the names of initiatives (projects and activities), information on indicators and executors of initiatives. The promising initiatives, projects and events of the Decade Plan include: the initiatives “Science is Nearby”, “Science of Winning”, “Scientific Volunteering”, “Third Semester”, “Working with Experience and Designing the Future”, “Science and Business”, “Popular Science Tourism”, “Science as Art”, “Solutions and Services for the Professional Community”, the “Scientific Children’s Playgrounds” project, thematic initiatives of the Decade of Science and Technology on the priorities of the Strategy for Scientific and Technological Development of the Russian Federation, as well as the Congress of Young Scientists. In 2024, over 12,000 federal and regional events were held as part of the Decade. More than 43 million people took part in the projects and events. As part of the implementation of the initiatives “Science Nearby”, “Science of Winning”, “Scientific Volunteering” and “Third Semester” over 200 lectures, open lessons and excursions to scientific laboratories were held. More than 2 million schoolchildren took part in the Olympiads. Over 100 scientific projects for young scientists were also implemented. The initiatives “Working with experience and designing the future” and “Science and business” made it possible to implement over 24 thousand start-up projects and demonstrate promising developments of young scientists. As part of the grand competition for the production of national cinema, a separate track was launched for the first time for films dedicated to modern Russian science and outstanding scientists of the past (the “Science as Art” initiative).  Also, 76 popular science tourist routes and 47 scientific playgrounds have been opened in the Russian Federation.  The IV Congress of Young Scientists brought together over 7,000 participants.  In 2025, within the framework of the Decade, work will continue on a number of projects, including: support for science fiction, development of a literature fund and creation of a special thematic library, integration of science into art, and development of the artistic image of a scientist in libraries, museums, theaters.  The Ministry of Science and Higher Education of the Russian Federation has developed and is implementing support measures aimed at popularizing science and scientific knowledge. For the first time in 2023, a competition was organized to support authors covering issues of science and technology. In March 2024, following the results of the grant competition for authors, 19 agreements were concluded. For the first time in 2023, a competition was organized to to support organizers of popular science events for a wide audience.
