# Country Profile: Uganda

*UNESCO 2021 Recommendation on Open Science — Survey Responses*

---

## Key Indicators

**R&D expenditure (% GDP):** The Gross domestic expenditure on R&D as a share of GDP in Uganda in the period 2020-2022 amounted to 0.14 percent. R&D spending as a percentage of the GDP has remained constant since 2020. In absolute terms, the country's gross spending on R&D amounted to 150 million U.S. dollars in 2022  (Saifaddin Galal, Sep 29, 2023,  https://www.statista.com/statistics/1345231/gerd-as-gdp-share-in-uganda/#:~:text=In%202022%2C%20the%20gross%20domestic,GDP%20amounted%20to%200.14%20percent).

**Internet access rate:** There were 13.30 million internet users in Uganda in January 2024. This translates to 27.0 percent of the total population that has access to the internet at the start of 2024 meaning that that 35.95 million people in Uganda which is 73.0 percent of the population remained offline at the beginning of the year 2024(https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2024-uganda#:~:text=Uganda's%20internet%20penetration%20rate%20stood,January%202023%20and%20January%202024). According to  the Permanent Secretary, Ministry of Information Communication and Technology (ICT),Internet users in Uganda showed steady increase from year to year, For example, internet users  increased by 1.2 million (10.3%) between January 2023 and January 2024 pushing  the total number of internet users in Uganda to 13.30 million(27.0% of total population by  Mar 2024.

### B3.3 — Please provide more information and links as relevant, and indicate to which open science actors in your country those networks are accessible

There is a Research and Education Network for Uganda (RENU), a not-for-profit National Research and Education Network (NREN) that promotes collaboration among its member institutions and their global partners. The network is a cooperatively owned and community-driven service provider that helps to facilitate research and education networking among its member institutions through interconnecting them with other research and education networks as well as to the commercial Internet worldwide, thereby overcoming the traditionally high costs of information and knowledge sharing and exchange. (https://www.kab.ac.ug/partner/research-and-education-network-for-uganda-renu/#:~:text=Research%20and%20Education%20Network%20for%20Uganda%20(RENU)%20is%20a%20not,institutions%20and%20their%20global%20partners) The website is: https://renu.ac.ug/ There is a National Research Information Management System (NRIMS) that is an integrated online platform designed to serve researchers and research users (https://nrims.uncst.go.ug/index.php?s%C3%BCperbetin+giri%C5%9F+sayfas%C4%B1+)

## B7 – International Cooperation

### B7.1 — How is your country promoting and facilitating international scientific collaborations on open science as outlined in the 2021 Recommendation on Open Science?

Wider dissemination id being conducted on the Recommendation on Open Science. This is to be followed by the following actions:  Encouraging international scientific collaborations, as one of the integral practices of open science and the most important driving factor for an intensive exchange of scientific knowledge and experience, as well as the paramount for the openness of science.  (ii)  Supporting the creation and maintenance of effective collaborative networks to exchange best open science practices and lessons learned from the design, development and implementation of open science policies, initiatives and practices.
